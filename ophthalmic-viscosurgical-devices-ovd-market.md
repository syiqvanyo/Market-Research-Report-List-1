<p><p>Ophthalmic Viscosurgical Devices (OVDs) are critical tools used during eye surgeries, particularly cataract procedures. They can be categorized into three main types: cohesive OVDs, which maintain a stable anterior chamber and provide optimal visibility; dispersive OVDs, designed to coat and protect intraocular surfaces while maintaining space; and combined OVDs, which leverage the benefits of both cohesive and dispersive properties to enhance surgical outcomes. Each type plays a vital role in ensuring safety and efficacy during ophthalmic surgeries.</p></p>
