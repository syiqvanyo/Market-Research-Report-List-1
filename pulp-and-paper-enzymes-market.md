<p><p>The pulp and paper enzymes market consists of various enzyme types that enhance the production process. Amylase is used to break down starch, improving paper strength. Cellulase helps in fiber degradation, boosting pulp quality and yield. Xylanase assists in breaking down hemicellulose, facilitating fiber separation and enhancing brightness. Lipase aids in the removal of fatty substances, improving paper surface quality. Other enzymes include ligninase and pectinase, which target additional components to optimize pulp processing and paper properties.</p></p>
