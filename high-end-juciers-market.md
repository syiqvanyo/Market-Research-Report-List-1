<p><p>The high-end juicers market is primarily divided into high-speed and low-speed juicers. High-speed juicers, often centrifugal, utilize rapid blade rotation to extract juice quickly but may generate heat, which can affect nutrient integrity. Conversely, low-speed juicers, typically masticating or triturating models, operate at slower speeds to preserve nutrients and enzymes, resulting in higher juice quality and yield. While high-speed juicers appeal to those seeking convenience, low-speed juicers attract health-conscious consumers prioritizing nutrient retention and flavor.</p></p>
