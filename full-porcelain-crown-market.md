<p><ul><li>Silica</li><li>Alumina</li><li>Zirconia</li><li>Monolithic Zirconia</li><li>Lithium-Disilicate</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The full porcelain crown market consists of several key types, each with distinct characteristics. Silica-based crowns offer aesthetic appeal but lower strength. Alumina crowns provide enhanced durability while still maintaining good aesthetics. Zirconia crowns are known for their strength and translucency, making them ideal for posterior restorations. Monolithic zirconia crowns enhance this strength further, suitable for high-stress areas. Lastly, lithium-disilicate crowns combine superior aesthetics with moderate strength, making them versatile for both anterior and posterior applications.</p></p>
