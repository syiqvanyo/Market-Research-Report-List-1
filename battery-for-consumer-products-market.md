<p><p>The consumer battery market is dominated by three main types: Lithium-Ion (Li-Ion) batteries, lead-acid batteries, and nickel batteries. Li-Ion batteries are favored for their high energy density and lightweight properties, making them ideal for portable electronics. Lead-acid batteries are commonly used for automotive applications due to their affordability and reliability, despite being heavier. Nickel batteries, including nickel-cadmium and nickel-metal hydride, offer good performance for specific applications but are less prevalent due to environmental regulations and competition from Li-Ion technology.</p></p>
