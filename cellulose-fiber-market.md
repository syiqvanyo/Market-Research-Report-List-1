<p><p>The cellulose fiber market is divided into two main types: natural cellulose fibers and man-made cellulose fibers. Natural cellulose fibers, derived from plants like cotton, hemp, and flax, are biodegradable and sustainable, making them popular in textiles and eco-friendly products. Man-made cellulose fibers, such as viscose and lyocell, are produced through chemical processes from wood pulp, offering versatility and a range of textures.
